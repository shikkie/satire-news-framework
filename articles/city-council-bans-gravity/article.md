In a marathon session that lasted until the snacks ran out, the City Council voted **9–0** to outlaw gravity inside municipal boundaries, effective “as soon as someone figures out the enforcement part.”

Councilmember **Rita Nimbus** called the measure “long overdue,” arguing that gravity had been “dragging the city down for too long — literally.”

> “We’re tired of things falling,” Nimbus said. “Leaves. Morale. Property values. This ends now.”

### What residents need to know

- Outdoor chairs must be tethered or “emotionally committed to staying put.”
- Coffee is reclassified as a controlled *horizontal* beverage until further notice.
- The Department of Public Works will issue free “downward momentum waivers” for elevators and toddlers.

Critics noted that gravity is a fundamental interaction and not, strictly speaking, a municipal service. City attorneys responded with a 40-page memo titled **“Jurisdiction Over the Obvious.”**

The mayor’s office released a statement promising a soft landing — then immediately walked it back, citing compliance concerns.
